# easyJet Audit Risk Review

Audit-style financial reporting and risk assessment project on easyJet plc, built for graduate applications in audit, accounting advisory, and transaction services.

## Project summary

This project reviews easyJet's FY2025 financial statements through an audit planning lens. It combines ratio analysis, raw financial data, audit flags, and IFRS-aware commentary to show how published financial statement information can be translated into practical audit focus areas.

## Why I built it

I built this project to demonstrate commercially grounded audit thinking rather than academic theory. The aim was to show that I can:

- work from published financial statements and notes
- identify realistic audit risks
- connect ratio movements to underlying accounting issues
- support commentary with defendable workings
- communicate clearly in a professional memo format

## Skills demonstrated

- Financial statement analysis
- Audit risk assessment and planning
- IFRS and accounting judgement awareness
- Evidence-led analytical review
- Excel-based workings and data organisation
- Python-supported document and workbook generation
- Professional written communication

## Key themes covered

- Revenue recognition and customer balances under IFRS 15
- Deferred revenue and revenue cut-off risk in an airline model
- Lease liabilities and right-of-use assets
- Liquidity and capital structure analysis
- Maintenance provision and estimate sensitivity
- Audit procedures, professional scepticism, and planning priorities
